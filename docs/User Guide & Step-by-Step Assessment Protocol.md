## üîç Detailed Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Basic Information Collection

#### 1.1 Navigate to "Body Composition" Tab
- Click the first tab labeled "Body Composition"
- Review the example data pre-filled for reference

#### 1.2 Enter Basic Information

Required Fields:
1. Height: Stand straight, heels together, measure without shoes
2. Weight: Use scale on hard surface, minimal clothing
3. Age: Current age in years
4. Sex: Select biological sex for accurate calculations

Measurement Tips:
- Height: Measure against a wall, book level on head
- Weight: Same scale, same time of day, same clothing
- Consistency: Always measure under similar conditions

**Example:**
- Height: 175 cm (5'9")
- Weight: 70 kg (154 lbs)
- Age: 30 years
- Sex: Male/Female

### Step 2: Circumference Measurements

#### 2.1 Waist Circumference
**Technique:**
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
2. Locate top of hip bone and bottom of rib cage
3. Place tape midway between these points
4. Ensure tape is horizontal and snug (not tight)
5. Measure at the end of normal expiration
6. Record to nearest 0.5 cm

**Common Errors to Avoid:**
- ‚ùå Measuring over clothing
- ‚ùå Pulling tape too tight
- ‚ùå Tape not horizontal
- ‚ùå Holding breath

#### 2.2 Hip Circumference
**Technique:**
1. Stand with feet together
2. Locate widest part of buttocks
3. Place tape at this level, horizontal
4. Record to nearest 0.5 cm

**Target Ranges:**
- Men: Waist < 94 cm (low risk)
- Women: Waist < 80 cm (low risk)

### Step 3: Bone Diameter Measurements (Optional)

#### 3.1 Wrist Diameter
**Location:** Distal end of radius and ulna (wrist joint)
**Technique:** Use calipers or flexible tape

#### 3.2 Knee Diameter
**Location:** Femoral condyles (knee joint line)
**Technique:** Measure with knee slightly bent

**Note:** These measurements improve bone mass estimation accuracy

### Step 4: Skinfold Measurements (Optional - Advanced)

#### 4.1 Required Tool: Skinfold Calipers
- Recommended: Harpenden, Lange, or Slim Guide calipers
- Accuracy: ¬±3-5% with proper technique

#### 4.2 Measurement Protocol
**General Technique:**
1. Pinch skin 1 cm from measurement site
2. Place calipers perpendicular to skin fold
3. Read measurement 2 seconds after release
4. Take 2-3 measurements, use average
5. All measurements in millimeters (mm)

#### 4.3 Site-Specific Techniques

**Triceps:**
- Location: Midpoint between acromion and olecranon
- Fold: Vertical fold
- Arm: Relaxed at side

**Subscapular:**
- Location: 1-2 cm below inferior angle of scapula
- Fold: Diagonal fold (45¬∞ downward)
- Body: Standing, arms relaxed

**Abdominal:**
- Location: 2-3 cm right of umbilicus
- Fold: Vertical fold
- Body: Standing, relaxed abdomen

**Thigh:**
- Location: Midpoint between hip and knee
- Fold: Vertical fold
- Leg: Weight on opposite leg

### Step 5: Health Metrics Entry

#### 5.1 Navigate to "Health Metrics" Tab
- Click the second tab
- Enter measurements from your health monitoring

#### 5.2 Required Health Metrics

1. Resting Heart Rate (RHR)
   - Measure: Morning, before rising
   - Method: Count pulses for 30 seconds √ó 2
   - Normal: 60-100 bpm (athletes: 40-60 bpm)

2. Blood Pressure
   - Position: Seated, arm supported
   - Timing: 5 minutes rest before measurement
   - Cuff: Appropriate size for arm

3. Blood Glucose
   - Timing: Fasting (8-12 hours no food)
   - Normal: 70-100 mg/dL (fasting)

4. Total Cholesterol
   - Fasting: Required for accurate measurement
   - Target: <200 mg/dL

#### 5.3 Activity Level Selection
Choose the category that best describes your weekly activity:

```
Sedentary: <30 min moderate activity/week
Light: 30-150 min moderate activity/week
Moderate: 150-300 min moderate activity/week
Active: >300 min moderate activity/week
Athlete: >450 min + structured training
```

```

**Squat Estimation:**
```

#### 6.3 Goal Setting
Select your primary fitness goal:
- Fat Loss
- Muscle Gain
- Maintenance
- Strength
- Cardiovascular Health

### Step 7: Generate Comprehensive Summary

#### 7.1 Navigate to "Comprehensive Summary" Tab
- Click the fourth tab
- Click "Generate Complete Assessment"

#### 7.2 Review Your Health Score
**Score Interpretation:**
- 4.5-5.0: Excellent health profile
- 3.5-4.4: Good health, minor improvements possible
- 2.5-3.4: Moderate health, some areas need attention
- <2.5: Needs improvement, consider professional consultation

#### 7.3 Review Risk Factors
Pay special attention to:
- High-risk classifications (red indicators)
- Multiple moderate-risk factors
- Trends over time (if available)

### Step 8: Exercise Prescription

#### 8.1 Navigate to "Exercise Prescription" Tab
- Click the fifth tab
- Click "Generate Full Prescription"

#### 8.2 Review Your Personalized Plan
**Components to Review:**
1. Daily calorie target
2. Timeframe for goals
3. Weekly targets
4. Cardiovascular prescription
5. Strength training prescription
6. Specific recommendations

#### 8.3 Implementation Tips
**Cardiovascular Training:**
- Start at lower end of recommended intensity
- Gradually increase duration before intensity
- Monitor heart rate during exercise

**Strength Training:**
- Focus on form first
- Progressive overload: Add weight when reps become easy
- Allow 48 hours between training same muscle groups

#### 9.3 Session Management

## üîß Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

#### 1. Measurements Not Saving
**Symptoms:**
- "Save Session" button doesn't work
- No confirmation notification
- Session doesn't appear in history

**Solutions:**
1. Check all required fields (highlighted in red if missing)
2. Ensure browser has storage permissions enabled
3. Try refreshing the page
4. Clear browser cache if persistent

#### 2. Calculation Errors
**Symptoms:**
- Incorrect BMI or other calculations
- Negative values
- Unrealistic results

**Solutions:**
1. Verify measurement units (cm vs. inches, kg vs. lbs)
2. Check decimal points
3. Ensure all measurements are within realistic ranges
4. Contact support if issue persists

#### 3. Display Issues
**Symptoms:**
- Charts not loading
- Table formatting problems
- Text overlapping

**Solutions:**
1. Update browser to latest version
2. Disable browser extensions temporarily
3. Use recommended browsers (Chrome, Firefox)
4. Check screen resolution settings

#### Minimum System Requirements

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Guidelines

### Medical Considerations

#### Contraindications
**Do NOT use this assessment if you have:**
- Acute illness or infection
- Uncontrolled hypertension (>180/110 mmHg)
- Recent surgery (<6 weeks)
- Active injury preventing movement
- Pregnancy (consult healthcare provider)

#### Red Flags Requiring Medical Attention
- Chest pain during assessment
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Severe shortness of breath
- Irregular heart rhythm
- Unexplained weight loss/gain

### Ethical Considerations

#### Privacy
- All data stored locally on your device
- No data transmitted to external servers
- Export/backup your own data regularly

#### Professional Boundaries
- This tool supplements, doesn't replace professional care
- Refer to healthcare providers for medical conditions
- Work with certified trainers for exercise programming

### Age Considerations

#### Adults (18-65 years)
- Full assessment protocol applicable
- Adjust expectations based on age norms
- Consider age-related changes

#### Older Adults (>65 years)
- May need modified protocols
- Focus on functional assessments
- Consider medical conditions

#### Adolescents (13-17 years)
- Parental supervision recommended
- Use age-adjusted norms
- Focus on healthy habits, not extremes
